About This Website

Introduction

The name Apparebit is the third-person singular future active indicative of the Latin verb appareo and translates to “it will appear.” It also is programmatic: This website started as a showcase for my photography and now also features my writing. To accommodate that mission growth, I redesigned Apparebit twice, with each subsequent version being more ambitious than the previous one.

History

  1. The first version of Apparebit featured a rotating cast of photographs under a couple of headings and was utterly brutalist long before brutalist design became fashionable. It also fell short on information architecture, semantic markup, responsiveness, and accessibility. It did, however, feature a deliberate choice of typeface, Helvetica, an hitherto unacknowledged nod towards my first conscious use of a typeface, Akzidenz-Grotesk Buch, for a series of posters promoting gay dance parties in my hometown Würzburg during the late 1980s.
  2. The second version, released in April 2012, was rather serious, presenting my photography as art. Around the time, I did indeed participate in a couple of group shows and also won an award for my work. Whereas the first version fell short on best practices, the second version made up for it and then some. It was semantic, accessible, and most definitely responsive—with supported browsers ranging from Internet Explorer on the desktop down to dedicated web views after smartphone installation. Alas, the visual design itself was bland, utilitarian, and, ahem, skeuomorphic—thanks to an overindulgence in fine shadows and highlights. Sadly, I removed this imitation of life only three years later in August 2015. Thereafter, the design was suitably bland, utilitarian, and flat. Huzzah!
  3. The need for another redesign became apparent a couple of years ago. I was increasingly using Facebook as a de facto blog. But I was also becoming increasingly concerned about blogging in that deeply suspect environment. Subsequent efforts at completely redesigning Apparebit to accommodate my writing in addition to my photography while simultaneously developing my own software for managing the website yielded considerable insight into overall architecture, visual appearance, typography, and necessary tooling. But the scope of the effort also was too ambitious to be conducive to completion. While working on a one-page side project, I realized that the difference between that self-contained page and a minimum viable Apparebit had shrunk to a couple of weeks work and went for it. I launched the third version of Apparebit in late August 2019 and made the source repository public in early September 2019. From here on out, I expect development for Apparebit to take place in the open.

Typography

Apparebit’s current design features typefaces by contemporary designers from Argentina, France, and the United States. Body text throughout this website is set in Bely by Roxane Gataud, which is available through TypeTogether. Headings and pullquotes vary between pages and, in addition to Bely Display, use the following typefaces:

  • Bild designed by David Jonathan Ross and released through his Font of the Month Club;
  • Bluu Suuperstar designed by Jean-Baptiste Morizot and released through Black Foundry;
  • Reforma 2018 designed by Alejandro Lo Celso and released under the Creative Commons BY-ND 4.0 license.

Finally, the website logo and icon use the stenciled version of Super Fat Bob also designed by Jean-Baptiste Morizot and released through FutureFonts.

FutureFonts and Ross’ Font of the Month Club are great resources for new type designs. Their typefaces may not come in as many variants as those in more conventional outlets and they may also be very much in-progress. But they are always interesting and fresh. They also tend to be affordable, while providing some measure of a steady income to independent type designers. A win-win for all parties involved!
