Privacy Notice

Overview

This privacy notice explains the data collection and processing practices relating to Apparebit. Seeing that I, Robert Grimm, reside in the United States of America and that this website is hosted by an American firm within the United States, the European Union’s General Data Protection Regulation only applies to visitors from the EU. Nonetheless, Apparebit’s entire data collection and data processing practices, independent of a visitor’s location, are directly informed by and fully compliant with GDPR. This privacy notice serves to meet the lawfulness, fairness and transparency requirements of that regulation and any other applicable laws and regulations.

Very much in line with GDPR’s principle of data minimisation, Apparebit does not collect any personal data. It does, however, maintain conventional server access logs and may also install code and data on your computer. The rest of this notice first explains the operation of this website, then discusses sharing its content to social networks, and finally outlines basic operational practices. If you have any questions or concerns about this notice or the practices described, please do not hesitate to reach out to me. After all, I am this website’s controller, responsible for both content and operation.

Accessing Content on Apparebit

Apparebit maintains conventional server access logs that identify what resources were accessed at what time and from what location. Logged information typically includes the URL, a timestamp with millisecond resolution, your computer’s IP address, and your browser. Apparebit collects that information for three reasons:

  • To better understand what content you access and how you interact with the content;
  • To comply with licensing requirements, notably for commercially licensed typefaces;
  • To ensure the security of this website and the integrity of its content.

The above reasons meet not only one but two of the requirements for lawful data processing as laid out in GDPR’s Article 6. Specifically, they are 1.c “compliance with a legal obligation” and 1.f “legitimate interests persued by the controller.” Yet, despite being well within the limits for lawful data collection, Apparebit does not collect any personal information. Furthermore, it does not track your online activities through, say, “cookies.” It also does not utilize third-party tracking and analytics services, such as Google Analytics.

If your browser supports it, Apparebit may install a small script, known as a “service worker,” on your computer. That script may, in turn, download resources from this website onto your computer. It does not collect any information about you, your computer, or your use of your computer. Its only purpose is to make your experience accessing Apparebit a more pleasant one. You can verify that claim yourself or have somebody with the requisite background do so for you, since the source code for Apparebit is open source.

Sharing Content from Apparebit

This website integrates with other websites, notably social networks, only minimally. In particular, Apparebit does not embed any third-party content or services. That is very much intentional because doing so would enable that third party to track Apparebit’s visitors as well.

This website does, however, link to content on other websites including on social networks. Apparebit’s privacy practices do not apply, if you follow such outbound links. Furthermore, this website does contain identifiers for my social network accounts on Facebook and Twitter. If you share content from this website to either social network, that network tracks your activity and makes aggregate information available to me.

Please consult the privacy policies for Facebook and Twitter for further details.

Operating Apparebit

In developing and operating Apparebit, I follow best security practices as much as possible. That includes using randomized passwords, two-factor authentication, and only encrypted network channels. The sources for Apparebit are stored on my own computers and on Github’s servers. I use my own static website generator called site:forge to produce the production version, which is then deployed to my hosting provider’s server. Logs are stored on that server machine and on my desktop computer. Access is restricted to myself only. Though I do reserve the right to share aggregate statistics with select third parties, as becomes necessary for development and operation of this site.
